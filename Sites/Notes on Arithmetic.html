Rounding

When doing calculations we are sometimes left with a decimal that is way too long to write down so we must cut it off at some point which will lose us information and accuracy however make the number more readable. When one rounds one should always use an approximately equal symbol (\(\approx\)) when one goes from a calculation/accurate representation of a number to a rounded one. With rounding you cut off information, so for example if you wanted someone to get a feeling for how large irrational numbers are, you'd have to round them since you can't provide them with the entire number (they are infinite after all). Let's say we want to round \(\sqrt{2}\) to the nearest 5th decimal. First let us write down 7 digits of \(\sqrt{2}\), making sure not to round at all but instead simply cut off the digits after, called truncation. So \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414213\). The rules for rounding are that from 0 to 4 you round down and from 5 to 9 you round up. In this case, if we want to round to the 5th decimal place, we would need to look at the decimal before it, which is 3, and since it is 3 we round down, so, rounded, \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421\) Let's look at an example of rounding up.
Round \(\sqrt{3}\) to the 6th decimal place. First we write the number with 7 digits after the decimal point: \(\sqrt{3} \approx 1.7320508\). We want to round to the 6th decimal so we look at the 7th and see that it is an 8, so we round up. So \(\sqrt{3} \approx 1.732051\).

Percentage

Percentage can be a bit of an abstract concept if you don't truly understand it. As a side note for mathematics as a whole: If you understand something you will also be able to apply and use it better. Same with percentage, which a lot of people struggle with. Math is not about memorizing formulae and computing things (ironic for me to say in the arithmetics chapter). The ideas in maths will continue getting more and more complex and abstract, so it is crucial to have an understanding of them and a way to relate them to things that are not abstract and make sense very intuitively.
Let's say you have a box of 20 apples. Percentage represents a part of something, a segment of something, with 100% being the entire thing and 0% being no part of it. For example, if we now eat half of the apples in the box we will be left with 10 apples. Since we started with 100% of the apples and now ate half of them it is equivalent to dividing by two. So, for 100% = 20 apples, \(100%\div 2\) = 50% = 10 apples. Let's say we want any percentage of a number, then we will get 1% of that number and then multiply it with any percent we want. For example, say we want to get 274% of a number, then we will first find 1% of the number and then multiply 1% with 274 to get 274%. And notice that getting from 100% to 274% is equivalent to multiplying with 2.74, and this works generally, that is, any percentage can be represented as either a percentage (%), or as a decimal. It is sort of hard to explain these things generally, but mostly solving percentage problems boils down to realising whether or not you have the whole amount (100%) or some other percentage of it (80% for example) and then working with that.
Lastly, let me mention compounding interest. This is a topic that will come up once more in the future. Let's say you put one dollar in a bank which gives you 50% interest twice a year. So we'd calculate that by starting with our 1 dollar, multiplying it with 1.5 since that is 150% (50% interest), and then multiplying the outcome of that with 1.5 again, mathematically: \(1 \cdot 1.5 \cdot 1.5\) or \( 1\cdot 1.5^2 = 2.25\), so after a year we'd have 2.25 bucks.
Another example: We have 40000 euros saved in our bank account and our bank has an annual interest rate of 3.4%. How much will we have in our bank account after 20 years?
Solution: Every year our balance increases by 3.4%, so is multiplied by 1.034, and since we wait for 20 years it will be multiplied by 1.034 20 times, so our balance after 20 years will be \(40000 \cdot 1.034^{20} = 40000 \cdot 1.9516897... \approx 40000 \cdot 1.952 = 78080\). Now, let us go over a few examples so you can get an intuitive understanding of how to work with percentages.

Examples

Example 1: What percent of 264 is 99?
Solution: Since 264 is 100%, we want to find which percentage of it is 99. We do this by dividing 99 by 1% of 264, which tells us how many percent of 264 the number 99 is. Try to make sense of this intuitively, why dividing 99 by 1% gives you the percentage. Dividing generally tells you how often something fits into another thing, so now we're asking how often 99 fits into 1%, which will give us the percentage that 99 has with respect to 100%, that is 264. 1% of 264 is 2.64, so \(\frac{99}{2.64} = 37.5\). Therefore, 99 is 37.5% of 264. We can double-check this by multiplying 264 with 0.375 to see that it is actually 37.5% of it. \(264 \cdot 0.375 = 99\).

Example 2: A class of 240 students has 117 female students. What percent of the class is male?
Solution: 240 is 100%, and we know that there are 117 female students. We assume the rest to be male (obviously), and from here on there are two ways to calculate which percentage of the class is male. Method 1: We figure out how many male students there are and then find the percentage of that with respect to 100%. Since there are 240 students in total and 117 female students than there are 240 - 117 = 123 male students. Then we divide 123 by 1% of 240, which is 2.4, so \(\frac{123}{2.4} = 51.25%\). Double-check: \(240 \cdot 0.5125 = 123\). Method 2: We figure out which percentage of the students are female and then subtract that from 100%. We do this the same way again, that is by dividing 117 by 1% which is 2.4, so \(\frac{117}{2.4} = 48.75%\), meaning that 48.75% of the students are female so the rest must be male, so 100% - 48.75% = 51.25%.

Example 3: A 350mL beaker is filled with a 12.8% acid solution, how many mL are acid?
Solution: 350mL is 100%, and we want to find what 12.8% of 350 is. Simple enough: \(350 \cdot 0.128 = 44.8\). So 44.8mL of the solution are acid.

Example 4: The price of a bicycle after a 25% discount is $679.99. What was the original price? Round to the nearest cent.
Solution: In this case we don't know 100%, since we don't know the original price, we only know the price after a 25% discount, and because of that we know 75%. Our goal is to find 100%, the original price. In this case, we have 75%, so 0.75 of the original price. We can find the original price by getting back to 100%, and to get from 0.75 to 1 we simply divide by 0.75, so 100% = \(\frac{679.99}{0.75} \approx 906.65\), so the original price of the bicycle is $906.65.

Example 5: You put 20 Euros in a bank account and get 0.4% interest every week, how much money will you have after two years? (1 year contains 52 weeks)
Solution: We start with 20 euros and every week we multiply with 1.004. Since there are 52 weeks in a year, and we wait for two years (2 * 52 = 104), our resulting balance after two years will be \(20 \cdot 1.004^{104} \approx 20 \cdot 1.515 = 30.3\).

Exercises

1.) Round to the 5th decimal: 3.14159265
2.) Round to the 3rd decimal: 1.2436485
3.) Round to the 4th decimal: 5.5555455
4.) Round to the 1st decimal: 0.999999
5.) Round to the 10th decimal: 9.9999999999999
6.) A jacket with original price of 120€ is discounted by 20% and a sales tax of 15% is added. What is the final price?
7.) A student scores 75% on a test. If 10 more correct answers would have raised the score to 80%, how many questions were on the test?
8.) A population of 10,000 grows by 5% annually. If the population decreases by 5% the following year, what is the net change in population after these two years?
9.) A company increases the price of its product by 25% and then offers a 20% discount. Is the final price higher or lower than the original, and by what percentage?
10.) A number is decreased by 30% and then increased by 30%. Is the final number equal to, greater than, or less than the original number? Calculate the exact percentage difference.

Answers

1.) 3.14159
2.) 1.244
3.) 5.5555
4.) 1
5.) 10
6.) \(120€ \cdot 0.8 \cdot 1.15 = 110.4€\)
7.) 10 = 5% \(\to\) 100% = 200
8.) \(10000 \cdot 1.05 \cdot 0.95 = 9975 \to \) Change in population is 25.
9.) Let's just give the product an arbitrary price like 1. So \(1 \cdot 1.25 \cdot 0.8 = 1\). Therefore, the price remains unchanged.
10.) Let's pick an arbitrary number like 1. \(1 \cdot 1.3 \cdot 0.7 = 0.91\). So the final number is less than the original number by 9%.
