Polynomial

A polynomial is a mathematical expression made up of variables and coefficients. "Poly" meaning many and "nomial" coming from "nomen" meaning name, or in our case term, so a polynomial is a sum of many terms.
Every polynomial takes the form of:

\[ \sum_{i=0}^n{a_ix^i} = a_1x^n + a_2x^{n-1} + \cdots +a_{n-1}x + a_n,\space\space n \in \mathbb{N}_0, a_i \in \mathbb{R}\]

Where \(a_i\) is a real coefficient. In words: A polynomial is a sum of terms where each term has a variable raised to a certain power multiplied with a coefficient. Note that this sum has a finite amount of terms, meaning a polynomial can have an arbitrary amount of terms, however not an infinite amount.
The degree of a polynomial, written \(deg(P)\), where P is a polynomial, tells you the highest power of a variable that can be found in P.
For example: \(For \space P = 2x^5 -3x^3 -x^2 + 5, deg(P) = 5\) because of the first term which has a 5 in the exponent.
